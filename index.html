I was born and raised in Tanzania, but my upbringing stretched across the country. I first grew up in Kilimanjaro—yes, Kilimanjaro, you read that right. The highest mountain in all of Africa was practically in my backyard :) Later, I moved to Arusha, a neighboring region, where I lived for two years. Afterwards, I relocated to Mwanza, in northern Tanzania. Known for Lake Victoria—the largest lake in Africa—Mwanza was home for three years. The lake is more than just water; it's a source of life for the area: recreation, fishing, transportation, and, most importantly, the origin of the Nile River, the longest river in Africa. Mwanza was the best. Next, I moved to Morogoro in central Tanzania. It was the greenest place I had ever seen besides Kilimanjaro. Surrounded by the beautiful Uluguru mountain ranges, trekking was always on my to-do list. Living there felt like being part of nature—it was simply beautiful. After Morogoro, I moved to Dar es Salaam—the New York of Tanzania. I admired how busy the city was. It was rare to find a day without something happening. At some point, I thought people never slept. But I liked it that way; I naively believed I'd always have something to do. As of now, my GPS is still set in Dar es Salaam.
